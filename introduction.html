Course Description and Learning Objectives

Course Description

Human Flourishing is a course about what it means to be human. This course, developed by the Center for Health and Well-Being at IE University, creates the space for experiential learning and growth to support learners in intentionally cultivating practices that proactively support human flourishing. As human beings, we have awareness of our existence, a multifaceted connection of body, mind, and soul, and a capacity for creativity, innovation, and learning. We are also hard-wired for human connection and for survival. This course takes a holistic approach to understanding our emotions, connections to values, thinking, behavior, and health to explore intentional practices that empower us to flourish for greater fulfillment and positive impact.

Positive Psychology

Key Point

Positive psychology is the scientific study of what makes life worth living. It focuses on the positive aspects of human experience, such as happiness, well-being, and flourishing. Rather than just addressing mental illness, positive psychology seeks to understand and promote the factors that contribute to a fulfilling and meaningful life.

Dr. Mario Alonso Puig on Human Flourishing

Well-Being

Key Point

Well-being encompasses both hedonic and eudaimonic aspects. Hedonic well-being focuses on pleasure and happiness, while eudaimonic well-being emphasizes meaning and purpose in life. True flourishing involves cultivating both dimensions to create a rich, fulfilling existence.

Human Connection

Key Point

The Harvard Adult Development Study, one of the longest-running studies on human happiness, has found that good relationships are the key to happiness and health. Quality relationships, not quantity, are what matter most for our well-being and longevity.

Initial Survey

Before we begin our journey together, we invite you to take a brief self-assessment. This survey uses validated well-being measures to help you reflect on your current state of flourishing. The results are not meant to serve as a judgment or final outcome. Instead, they offer a starting point for reflection—a snapshot of where you are today that you can revisit as you progress through the course.

Remember, flourishing is a gradual, personal process. Be honest with yourself and approach this assessment with curiosity rather than self-criticism.
