Course Description and Learning Objectives

Course Description

Human Flourishing is a course about what it means to be human. This course, developed by the Center for Health and Well-Being at IE University, creates the space for experiential learning and growth to support learners in intentionally cultivating practices that proactively support human flourishing. As human beings, we have awareness of our existence, a multifaceted connection of body, mind, and soul, and a capacity for creativity, innovation, and learning. We are also hard-wired for human connection and for survival. This course takes a holistic approach to understanding our emotions, connections to values, thinking, behavior, and health to explore intentional practices that empower us to flourish for greater fulfillment and positive impact.

A Special Welcome from Mario Alonso Puig

What Does Well-Being Mean?

Key Point

Well-Being vs. Wellness: Well-being and wellness are often used interchangeably, but they have distinct meanings. Wellness is more focused on physical care for ourselves. In contrast, well-being considers a more integral combination of body, mind, and soul and encompasses the elements of balance, appreciation, and perspective.

A Brief Background on Positive Psychology

Key Point

Positive psychology puts forth the idea that remedying the "bad" in our lives is not the same as cultivating the "good." As opposed to focusing on fixing problems in our lives to get to a "neutral" state, a goal of positive psychology is to empower individuals to go beyond a neutral state to cultivate a flourishing state.

A Word on the Visual Design of this Course

The visual design of this course on Human Flourishing was carefully created to reflect the course's values and learning objectives.

As students will note, the central image of this course is that of a fingerprint, reminding us of what it means to be human and that each of us has a unique human identity.

This fingerprint is also linked to the image of a labyrinth, which recalls the complexities of being human and the challenges we may have to navigate in our journey toward flourishing. However, if we persist on this journey, we will certainly learn valuable lessons about ourselves and others.

Finally, the images of the fingerprint and the labyrinth are contextualized within nature, reminding us that our human identity is linked to the natural world. Of course, the concept of flourishing also recalls images of the growth of plants, trees, and other living things.

We hope that this visual design adds meaning to your experience in this course. Enjoy!
