Living with a Sense of Purpose

Key Point

We can cultivate a sense of purpose by first recognizing that we are continuously learning and that the nature of purpose can evolve over time. As we consider our purpose, we should identify what is truly important to us so that we can prioritize it accordingly and, ultimately, tap into a better understanding of our values.

Activity: Finding Your Ikigai

"Ikigai" is a Japanese concept that means "a reason for being" or "a purpose in life." It is a combination of four Japanese words: "iki" (life), "kai" (the realization of hopes and expectations), "ga" (self), and "ai" (worth or value). This concept is often used as a framework for personal development and goal setting.

As seen in the diagram below, the concept of ikigai suggests that when you can find the intersection between what you love, what you are good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for, you will have found your "reason for being" or "purpose in life."

By tapping into what is really important in our lives, we can better align our priorities and daily behaviors for greater fulfillment.

The Self-Determination Theory

Key Point

The Self-Determination Theory is made of up three parts: autonomy, relatedness, and competence. Autonomy allows us to feel like we have ownership over our lives. Relatedness highlights our need for connection to our communities and the world around us. Competence refers to our ability to learn and succeed. All three of these elements are critical for our motivation.

Extrinsic Motivation vs. Intrinsic Motivation

Key Point

Extrinsic motivation is driven by external factors like societal expectations and validation from others, which can erode a sense of meaning over time since the driving force behind our motivation does not come from within ourselves. In contrast, intrinsic motivation, which comes from within and is more aligned with our personal values, is associated with a greater sense of purpose and lasting satisfaction.
