The Appreciative Inquiry Framework

Key Point

The Appreciative Inquiry Framework is made up of four elements: Discover, Dream, Design, and Destiny. The Discover phase allows for an appreciation and understanding of self; the Dream phase allows us to create a vision for the future; the Design phase allows us to put our practices into place; and the Destiny phases allows us to exert ownership in our practice.

Activity: Applying the Appreciative Inquiry Framework

Let's further apply the Appreciative Inquiry Framework to our own lives.

Grab your journal or a notebook and go through these four quick steps — one for each stage of the AI cycle:

1

Discover:

Think of a recent moment when you felt proud, energized, or truly at your best.

Write down: What were you doing? What strengths were you using?

2

Dream:

Now imagine a future where you live and work like that more often.

Write down: What would your ideal day look like if that "best self" showed up regularly?

3

Design:

What small habit or change could you introduce to move toward that dream?

Write down: One action you can take this week.

4

Destiny:

What's one way you'll keep this momentum going?

Write down: How will you remind yourself to keep growing in that direction? (e.g., a sticky note, a calendar reminder, a mantra)

The PERMA Model

Key Point

The PERMA Theory of Well-Being, created by Dr. Martin Seligman, defines key elements that enable flourishing: Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishment. By building strategies that allow us to promote PERMA in our daily lives, we can take further ownership over our flourishing.

Activity: Applying the PERMA Model

Let's work to further integrate PERMA in our daily lives.

Take your journal or notebook and go through the following questions around each of the elements of the PERMA framework.

1

Positive Emotions:

Can you describe the best thing that happened to you yesterday?

2

Engagement:

Can you share a strength that characterizes you and how you use it in your life?

3

Relationships:

Can you name one person that makes your life better?

4

Meaning:

Can you name a cause that inspires you?

5

Accomplishment:

Can you describe one thing that took you out of your comfort zone but you did anyway?
