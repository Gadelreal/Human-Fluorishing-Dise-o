How Can We Define Resilience?

Key Point

We can consider an "evolved" definition of resilience to be the capacity to recognize what is needed and respond in the most appropriate way, considering the learning that comes with the experience. In this sense, resilience is not about enduring pressure at all costs, but about maintaining balance and recognizing when to take breaks or adapt approaches.

What Are Character Strengths and How Do They Support Resilience?

Key Point

Our character strengths are natural tendencies that we have that support our vitality. The VIA Institute on Character provides us with 24 research-backed character strengths, grouped into 6 "virtues." We can leverage these strengths to build our resilience in times of adversity.

Overcoming Procrastination

Key Point

Procrastination can't simply be brushed off as laziness: it's rooted in a feeling of discomfort with a task, often due to a feeling that we lack the capacity to complete that task. We can overcome procrastination by leveraging our strengths as a way to energize us as well as by chunking the task into smaller, more manageable parts.
